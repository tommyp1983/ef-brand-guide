Foundation

The Macro Thesis

Australia is staring down structural problems in founder-led businesses. Exit Forward addresses three interconnected challenges.

The Handover Problem

A wave of baby boomer founders are reaching a life stage where time becomes precious. They've built solid trades and SMEs over 30 to 40 years. The business is their superannuation. Default options feel bad: sell to a stranger via a broker, wind it down, or hope a family member wants it. None preserve what they built.

The Ceiling Problem

A larger group of founders, of any age, have built something solid and hit a wall. They've outgrown their own capacity. The business runs through them. They don't know how to scale beyond where it currently is. They're not retiring. They're stuck.

The Capital Lockout

A generation of capable, motivated young operators can't get on the property ladder, let alone buy a business. The capital gap between ready to run something and able to afford something has never been wider.

Exit Forward Closes the Gap

We partner with founders who need to break through, scale through, or hand over. We bring smart capital, a proven growth engine, and the operating experience to make it work. Where it fits, we match outgoing founders with capable incoming operators we've curated.

Definition

Equity Growth Partner

We take an equity stake. That's the deal. Founders get liquidity now without a full exit. We work alongside them to grow what's there. We bring capital, the systems we used to scale Dilate, and the experience of having actually sold a business.

Core Principle

Equity is non-negotiable. Every Exit Forward engagement involves us taking equity. Always. We are not advisors-for-hire. We are not consultants. We are not a fractional GM. If equity isn't on the table, there is no deal.

Market

Two Personas

Exit Forward markets to founders. Full stop. Two distinct personas, both builders, both hitting walls.

Persona 1

Boomer Founder

Trigger: Life stage. Realisation of mortality and preciousness of time.

Common triggers:

  • Kids growing up, missed key moments
  • Friend gets suddenly ill or dies
  • Marriage strain from work overcommitment
  • Imbalance. Time is finite.
Persona 2

Ceiling Founder

Trigger: Capability gap. Built something solid, hit a wall.

The reality:

  • Business runs through them
  • Tried scaling, hiring, consultants
  • Hit the same wall every time
  • Knows something needs to change

Right Founder (Applies to Both)

  • Self-reflective. Curious. Lifelong learner.
  • Wants to build a company, not just a job.
  • Open to structure and accountability.
  • Hitting a wall and knows something needs to change.
  • Open to vulnerability and trust.

Hard Exclusions

  • Equity-averse founders. Want growth support but won't part with equity. Not our model.
  • Fast exit seekers. Under 24-month horizon. Broker-style expectations.
  • Advice-only shoppers. Want consulting hours, not partnership.
  • Chaos operators. No appetite for structure or governance.

The Beer and Burger Test

Would Bodie and Tom enjoy a beer and a burger with this person? Cultural fit, energy and values alignment come before commercial terms. Always.

How It Works

Deal Patterns

Every deal involves equity. That's the floor. Patterns describe the shape of liquidity and involvement on top of that.

1. Growth-Led

Capital and partnership in. Founder liquidity is minimal or deferred. The goal is to break the ceiling and grow what's there. Common with Ceiling Founders who don't need cash now and want a partner to scale with.

2. Partial Liquidity

Founder takes cash off the table now, retains majority or significant equity, keeps building. Works for both personas. Removes pressure while preserving upside and control.

3. Succession

Staged handover, often with an internal 2IC stepping into operational leadership backed by our capital and structure. Common with Boomer Founders. Possible with Ceiling Founders where a 2IC has matured.

Most deals blend two patterns. The discovery conversation finds the right shape. The constant: Exit Forward holds equity in every deal.

Operating Constraints

Equity Structure

  • Equity is non-negotiable. Entry condition on every deal.
  • 50%+ is preferred. Structural position of choice for clarity and control.
  • Below 50% is negotiable. For the right deal and right operator, willing to take minority positions.
  • Hard constraint: operator sentiment. Equity split must track actual work and contribution, not just capital deployed. A resentful operator is a failing deal. Structure to protect psychology first, economics second.
Visual Language

Brand Imagery

Photography is monochrome, high contrast, architectural. It carries the brand's calm authority — skylines, facades, industry, landscape. Never stock-smiling-in-a-boardroom. Never AI illustration.

Preferred Subjects

  • Skylines at dusk and architectural geometry
  • Industrial detail — facades, structure, scale
  • Mountain and landscape forms
  • Moments of clarity, distance and transition

Treatment

Full-bleed images with an 80% black gradient protection at the bottom. Bebas white headlines sit in the protected area. The result is cinematic, never decorative.

Voice

How We Sound

Calm. Confident. Human. Understated.

Copy reads like it was written by an operator who has already made the decision you're considering. First-person plural for the firm. Second person for the founder. Founders are named — Bodie and Tom — not hidden behind "our team".

Avoid

  • Hype: "unlock", "10x", "revolutionary", "game-changing", "transform"
  • Urgency: "now", "don't miss", "limited time"
  • Consulting cues: "leverage", "synergies", "solutions", "offerings"
  • "Help" language — Exit Forward doesn't help, it partners
  • Emoji. Ever.
